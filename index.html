The Daffodil is a genre of the Amaryllidaceae family originally from the Mediterranean basin and Europe. Ig includes numerous bulbous species, most of the spring flowering, although there are some species that flouris in autumn.

The majority of their species are native to the Mediterranean region, but a few species are found throughout Central and China Asia.

The Daffodil are classified as divisions, which cover a wide range of shapes and colors. Like other family members, the Daffodil produce various alkaloids, which provide some protection to the plant, but can be poisonous if they are accidentally ingested.

Medical properties

The property of the alkaloids has been exploited for medicinal purposes in traditional medicine and has resulted in the production of galantamine for the treatment of Alzheimer's dementia.

Dahlia

The Dahlia is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae. It is the national flower of Mexico. The first name given to this flower in the Nahuatl lenguage was Atlcocotlixochitl, which comes from: a-tl (water), coco-tli (tube)and xochitl (flower), and which means "flower of the water stems". The Dahlia blooms in july and September.

Medical properties

The Dahlia contains inulin, which is a prebiotic, which benefits digestion in addition to collaborating in the assimilation of certain minerals such as calcium and magnesium. It is also a safe food source, it helps reduce triglycerides and cholesterol as well as lose weight.
